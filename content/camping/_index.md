Camping is central to the Scouting program and one of the most rewarding experiences Troop 90 offers. Our Scouts spend nights under the stars across all four seasons, building skills and friendships that last a lifetime.

## Why Camping Matters

### Leadership Development

Scouts take on real responsibility outdoors — leading hikes, setting up camp, and making decisions when conditions change. They learn firsthand that leadership means owning both successes and failures.

### Teamwork and Collaboration

Setting up tents, preparing meals, and gathering firewood all require coordination. Living and working together in the field teaches communication, trust, and how to resolve differences constructively.

### Confidence and Resilience

Facing the dark, navigating unfamiliar terrain, and recovering from mistakes builds genuine confidence. Scouts discover that setbacks are opportunities to grow.

### Independence and Self-Reliance

Hands-on skills — fire-building, cooking, navigation — foster self-reliance that serves Scouts long after they leave the trail.
